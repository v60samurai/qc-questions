# MQC Presets

The **minimally-qualified candidate (MQC)** is the candidate sitting at the assessment's cut score — neither well above nor well below it. Difficulty estimation depends on who the MQC is. Pick the preset closest to your assessment context, or write a new one and contribute it back.

Pass via the slash command: `--mqc "<one-liner>"`.

---

## Screening / pre-employment

### IT services (entry-level analyst, India)
> Entry-level IT-services candidate at the screening cut for an Indian services firm. English-medium schooling, BE/B.Tech or equivalent, conversational professional English required. Has encountered standard grammar rules in school but trips on edge cases like intervening prepositional phrases for SVA, reported-speech back-shift, register matching for business email.

### IT services (service-desk / customer support)
> Entry-level service-desk candidate at the screening cut. English-medium Indian schooling, comfortable with day-to-day customer-facing English. Knows basic grammar but unfamiliar with formal-register choices and reported-speech back-shift.

### Investment banking analyst (campus hire)
> Final-year Tier-1 commerce/economics undergrad at the screening cut for a sell-side analyst role. Strong arithmetic and financial literacy; conversant with NPV/IRR mechanics but new to multi-step DCF construction.

### Consulting / generalist case
> Final-year top-tier campus hire at the case-round cut. Quantitatively sharp, strong English, frameworks-fluent but not yet experienced with ambiguity-heavy real-world cases.

### Sales (B2B SaaS, IC role)
> 2–3 years of B2B SaaS sales experience at the hiring cut. Fluent in MEDDICC/SPICED basics; confident on objection handling for known objections but slower on novel ones.

## Academic admissions

### GMAT-style verbal (550 cohort cut)
> Adult test-taker at the 550 GMAT score cut. Comfortable with standard grammar and reading comprehension at college level; trips on idiom misuse, comparison errors, and inference questions that require holding 3+ premises in working memory.

### NEET-PG (cut for state quota)
> Indian MBBS graduate at the NEET-PG state-quota cut. Recalls high-yield clinical facts confidently; trips on second-line drug choices, atypical presentations, and image-based questions outside the standard textbook corpus.

### CAT verbal section (85th percentile cut)
> Working professional with 2–3 years' experience targeting the 85th-percentile CAT cut. Strong on para-jumbles and grammar but slower on inference-heavy RC and on para-summary questions that test paraphrasing.

### SAT verbal (1300 cut, US high school)
> US high-school junior at the 1300 composite cut. Comfortable with school-level grammar and basic literary analysis; trips on vocabulary-in-context questions involving formal/archaic register.

## Professional certification

### CFA Level I (first-attempt pass cut)
> First-attempt CFA Level I candidate at the pass cut. Comfortable with TVM, ratios, basic ethics; trips on derivatives pricing edge cases and FRA quirks (LIFO vs FIFO inventory under IFRS).

### PMP (first-attempt pass)
> Candidate with 3 years of project work at the PMP first-attempt pass cut. Knows PMBOK process groups; trips on situational questions where two answers feel right and the test wants the most-correct-per-PMBOK.

### AWS Certified Solutions Architect — Associate
> Engineer with 1–2 years of AWS hands-on at the SAA-C03 pass cut. Comfortable with EC2/S3/RDS/VPC basics; trips on cost-optimization choices when two services overlap (e.g., SQS vs SNS for a given pattern).

## Secondary / school

### Indian CBSE Class 10 board (passing-with-distinction cut)
> Class 10 CBSE student targeting >85% marks. Solid on syllabus content; trips on application-level questions that require connecting two chapters.

### UK GCSE English Language (grade 5 cut)
> Year-11 student at the GCSE grade-5 cut. Reads at age-appropriate level; trips on inference-heavy unseen passages and on persuasive-language identification.

## How to choose

When in doubt, write the MQC as "the candidate whose true ability θ equals the assessment's pass threshold." Add the *specific knowledge baseline* and the *specific edge cases they're likely to miss*. The more concrete, the more reliable the Modified Angoff estimate.

If your assessment serves multiple cut scores (e.g., L1 vs L2 within the same test), define separate MQCs and pass the right one per section.
